## Operations Center

The Operations Center screen gives you a high-level view of your maintenance activity and quick links into the main modules.

### What this screen is for

The Operations Center is the main starting point for daily work.  
It brings together key counts, cards, and shortcuts so you can see what needs attention and jump into the right screen.

### Who typically uses it

Operations managers  
Maintenance supervisors  
Dispatchers  
Leaders who want a quick overview  

### Key things you can do

• See a summary of open and recent work  
• Spot overdue or high-priority jobs  
• Click into Work Orders, Dispatch, or other modules from one place  
• Use it as your “home base” at the start of the day  

### Create a new item from Operations Center

1. Open **Operations** from the left sidebar.  
2. Use the visible buttons or links (such as **Create Work Order**) to jump to the right module.  
3. Follow the creation steps in that module’s guide (for example, **Create a new work order**).  

### Layout overview

**Header**  
Shows the page title and a short description.

**Summary cards or sections**  
Highlight important numbers such as open work, overdue jobs, or other key metrics.

**Quick links**  
Provide buttons or links that take you directly to modules like **Work Orders**, **Dispatch**, or **Reports**.

### Typical workflow

Start day in Operations Center  
→ Review summary cards and counts  
→ Click into Work Orders, Dispatch, or another module  
→ Take action (assign work, update status, plan schedule)  
→ Return to Operations Center as needed to re-check the big picture  

### Tips

• Use Operations Center as your first stop each day to see what matters most.  
• Look for overdue or high-priority items and handle those first.  
• Train new users to start here instead of jumping straight to detailed lists.  

## Operations Center

### What this module is for

The Operations Center is the main landing page for day-to-day activity.  
It gives you a high-level view of work, often including dashboards and quick links into other modules.

### Who uses this module

Operations managers  
Maintenance supervisors  
Executives who want an overview

### When you would use this module

- Starting your day and seeing overall activity  
- Quickly jumping into Dispatch, Work Orders, or other areas  
- Reviewing key counts or summary information

### Key things you can do here

- Open the Operations Center at `/operations`  
- See summary blocks or cards that highlight important information  
- Use built-in links to move into more detailed modules

### Main screen overview

When you click **Operations Center** (or land there after sign-in) you arrive at `/operations`.  
This page is laid out as a dashboard with sections that summarize key information, such as work orders and important activity.  
Buttons and links on this page act as shortcuts into detailed modules like Dispatch, Work Orders, or Reports.

### Step-by-step guide

#### Start your day in Operations Center

1. After logging in, go to **Operations Center** if you are not there already.  
2. Review the main cards and counts to understand today’s workload.  
3. Use the links or buttons on the page to move into the area that needs attention first (for example, **Dispatch** or **Work Orders**).

### Common workflows

Operations Center overview  
↓  
Identify problem areas (for example, overdue work)  
↓  
Click into a detailed module  
↓  
Take action and then return to Operations Center as needed

### Tips and best practices

- Treat the Operations Center as your “home base” for daily coordination.  
- Use it to quickly decide where to focus—Dispatch, Work Orders, or another module.  
- Encourage supervisors to check it regularly rather than jumping directly into narrow views.

### Related modules

Work Orders  
Dispatch  
Reports  
Assets

### Notes for demos or onboarding

- Begin product demos from the Operations Center to show a high-level view first.  
- Then move into one or two modules (such as Work Orders and Dispatch) to show how users can act on what they see there.
