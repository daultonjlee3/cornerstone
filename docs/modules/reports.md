## Reports

The Reports screens turn your maintenance data into easy-to-read summaries so you can see how things are going over time.

### What this screen is for

Reports help you review work that has been done and spot patterns.  
You can see counts, trends, and detailed tables of work orders and other activity.

### Who typically uses it

Operations managers  
Maintenance supervisors  
Executives and leadership  

### Key things you can do

• Open standard reports from the **Reports** menu  
• Use **Operations Intelligence** for deeper operational views  
• Filter by date, status, or other options (where available)  
• Drill down from summaries into detailed records  

### Create a new report view

Reports are based on existing data; you do not “create” reports the way you create work orders.  
To see meaningful reports:

1. Make sure teams are regularly creating and closing work orders.  
2. Open **Reports** or **Operations Intelligence** from the sidebar.  
3. Adjust filters and date ranges to show the period and data you care about.  

### Layout overview

**Reports index (`/reports`)**  
Lists available report types and links into each one.

**Operations Intelligence (`/reports/operations`)**  
Shows more detailed operational views, often with filters and summary cards.

**Report pages**  
Combine filters, summary numbers, and tables you can scroll through or export.

### Typical workflow

Daily work orders and activity recorded  
→ Data flows into Reports and Operations Intelligence  
→ Leaders review key metrics and problem areas  
→ Teams adjust staffing, priorities, or plans based on what they see  

### Tips

• Pick a regular time (for example, weekly) to review reports with your team.  
• Start with a few simple questions, such as “How many jobs are overdue?” or “How fast do we close high-priority work?”.  
• When you see an issue, click into the underlying work orders and take concrete action there.
