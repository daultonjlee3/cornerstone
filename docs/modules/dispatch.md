## Dispatch

The Dispatch screen shows the scheduling board where you assign work orders to technicians and plan the day.

### What this screen is for

The Dispatch screen helps you see open work and technician schedules in one place.  
You use it to drag work orders onto technicians or crews and adjust the plan as the day changes.

### Who typically uses it

Dispatchers  
Maintenance supervisors  
Operations managers  

### Key things you can do

• View unscheduled work orders in a queue  
• See technician or crew lanes with time slots  
• Drag work orders onto a lane to assign and schedule them  
• Filter by date, technician, company, or other fields  
• Open a work order drawer for quick details  

### Create a new scheduled assignment

1. From the left sidebar, go to **Dispatch**.  
2. Find an unscheduled work order in the queue on the left.  
3. Click and drag the work order onto a technician or crew lane at the time you want.  
4. Release the mouse to drop it into that slot.  
5. Confirm any prompts if they appear.  

### Layout overview

**Top bar**  
Shows the date controls, filters, and view switches (for example, technicians vs. crews).

**Queue panel**  
Lists unscheduled or unassigned work orders that still need a spot on the schedule.

**Timeline with lanes**  
Each lane represents a technician or crew. Time runs across the top.  
Cards on a lane show the work orders assigned to that person or team.

### Typical workflow

Work order created  
→ Appears in the Dispatch queue  
→ Dispatcher drags it onto a technician or crew lane  
→ Technician completes the work  
→ Status is updated in Work Orders and reflected back in Dispatch  

### Tips

• Keep an eye on lane density so you do not overload one technician.  
• Use filters to focus on one company, technician, or priority when planning.  
• When priorities change, drag and drop cards to rebalance the day.  

## Dispatch

### What this module is for

The Dispatch module is your scheduling board for work orders.  
It helps you see open work, match it to technicians, and plan the day.

### Who uses this module

Dispatchers  
Maintenance supervisors  
Operations managers

### When you would use this module

- Planning the daily or weekly schedule  
- Assigning work orders to technicians or crews  
- Seeing which technicians are busy and which have open time  
- Rebalancing work when priorities change

### Key things you can do here

- Open the Dispatch board at `/dispatch`  
- View work in a queue, on a time grid, and in technician lanes  
- Drag work orders to assign and schedule them (where enabled)  
- See technician workload and availability  
- Optionally open a full-screen dispatch view

### Main screen overview

When you open **Dispatch** from the sidebar you see a large scheduling view.  
The screen is organized into panels such as:

- A queue of unscheduled work orders  
- Lanes for each technician or crew with time slots  
- Filters and date controls along the top  
- Optional side panels for map or insights, depending on configuration

You can usually switch between **Work Orders** and **Technician Queue** views inside Dispatch using tabs near the top.

### Step-by-step guide

#### Assign a work order from the queue

1. Click **Dispatch** in the left sidebar.  
2. Make sure the view is set to show the queue and technician lanes.  
3. Find the work order you want to schedule in the queue panel.  
4. Click and drag the work order onto a technician’s lane at the desired time slot.  
5. Release the work order to assign and schedule it.  
6. Confirm any prompts that appear.

#### Focus on a single technician

1. On the Dispatch page, use the technician filter at the top (if available).  
2. Choose the technician you want to focus on.  
3. The board updates to show only that technician’s schedule and related work.

### Common workflows

New work order created  
↓  
Appears in Dispatch queue  
↓  
Dispatcher schedules it onto a technician lane  
↓  
Technician completes the work  
↓  
Work order is closed and shows as completed

### Tips and best practices

- Use Dispatch to see the “big picture” of the day across all technicians.  
- Keep urgent work visible by using filters and sorting options where available.  
- Avoid overloading one technician; spread high-priority jobs across the team.
