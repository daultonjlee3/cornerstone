## Purchase Orders

The Purchase Orders screen tracks orders you place with vendors for parts, materials, or services.

### What this screen is for

The Purchase Orders screen helps you follow orders from creation through receiving.  
Each row is a purchase order with information like number, vendor, and status.

### Who typically uses it

Procurement and purchasing staff  
Inventory managers  
Accounts payable  
Operations managers  

### Key things you can do

• View a list of purchase orders  
• Open an order to see its details  
• Work with purchase order templates (where available)  
• Check which orders are open, received, or completed  

### Create a new purchase order

1. From the left sidebar, go to **Purchase Orders**.  
2. Click the **New Purchase Order** or **Create** button if it is available.  
3. Select the vendor you are ordering from.  
4. Add the products or items you want to buy.  
5. Review quantities and any notes, then click **Save** or **Create**.

### Layout overview

**Header**  
Shows the page title and main actions such as creating a new order or opening templates.

**Order list**  
Each row is a purchase order with key fields like number, vendor, and status.  
Click a row to open full order details.

**Order detail**  
Shows line items, totals, and any related information for that order.

### Typical workflow

Need identified from Inventory or work  
→ Purchase order created and sent to a vendor  
→ Items are delivered and marked as received  
→ Inventory is updated using those items  

### Tips

• Keep vendor and product records accurate to make building orders faster.  
• Use clear descriptions so both your team and the vendor know exactly what is being ordered.  
• Update order status when items are received so everyone knows what is still outstanding.
